Privacy Policy

How Creations Dominique Forand Inc. collects, uses and protects your personal information.

Last updated: May 12, 2026

1. Who we are

Creations Dominique Forand Inc. (“Dominique Forand”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) is a coaching and advisory business based in Montreal, Quebec, Canada. This policy explains how we collect, use, store and protect personal information through our website at dominiqueforand.com and through our coaching engagements.

We comply with the Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector (Quebec, Law 25), the federal Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA), and Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL).

3. Information we collect

We collect personal information that you provide directly and information collected automatically when you use our site.

Information you provide

  • Name, email address, phone number, and company information when you book an ALIGN session or contact us.
  • Billing details when you purchase a program, product or service (processed by our payment provider; we do not store full card numbers).
  • Information you share with us during coaching engagements, including business, financial, strategic and personal context relevant to the work.

Information collected automatically

  • IP address, browser type, device type, pages visited and referring URL, collected via standard server logs and analytics.
  • Cookies and similar technologies (see section 7).

4. Why we collect this information

  • To schedule and deliver the ALIGN session and any coaching program you engage.
  • To process payments, send invoices and meet our accounting and tax obligations.
  • To communicate with you about your booking, your engagement, and program updates.
  • To send marketing emails — only with your express consent, and only until you withdraw it (see section 8 on CASL).
  • To improve the site and our offers, in aggregate and de-identified form where possible.

5. Who we share it with

We do not sell personal information. We share it only with the service providers required to operate the business, under written agreements that bind them to confidentiality and use limitations:

  • Hosting and website infrastructure (Framer).
  • Payment processing (e.g. Stripe, SamCart).
  • Calendar and scheduling (e.g. Calendly).
  • Email delivery and CRM tools (e.g. Google Workspace and our email service provider).
  • Professional advisors (accounting, legal) bound by professional obligations of confidentiality.

Certain providers may store data outside Quebec or Canada (in particular in the United States and the European Union). Where this is the case, we have taken reasonable steps to ensure protection equivalent to that required under Quebec Law 25.

6. Retention

We keep personal information only as long as needed for the purposes above, then destroy or anonymize it. Typical retention periods:

  • Booking and contact form submissions: up to 24 months after last interaction.
  • Client records and coaching notes: for the duration of the engagement and 7 years thereafter (to meet professional and tax record-keeping requirements).
  • Billing and tax records: 7 years, as required by Canadian tax law.
  • Marketing email list: until you unsubscribe or we identify your address as inactive.

7. Cookies

Our site uses a small number of cookies and similar technologies. Strictly necessary cookies (for example to remember your language preference) are always active. Optional analytics or marketing cookies are only loaded with your consent.

You can refuse or delete cookies through your browser settings. Disabling strictly necessary cookies may affect site functionality.

8. Marketing emails and CASL

Under Canada’s Anti-Spam Legislation, we will only send you commercial electronic messages with your consent (express or implied as defined by CASL). Every marketing email contains a one-click unsubscribe link. To withdraw consent at any time, click that link or email us at the address above.

9. Your rights

Under Quebec Law 25 and PIPEDA you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Withdraw your consent at any time (for marketing or any other consent-based use).
  • Request that we de-index, de-reference or stop disseminating information about you under the conditions set out in Quebec Law 25.
  • Receive a portable copy of the information you provided to us in a structured, commonly used technological format (data portability).
  • File a complaint with the Commission d’accès à l’information du Québec (cai.gouv.qc.ca) or the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada (priv.gc.ca) if you believe your rights have been infringed.

To exercise any of these rights, email the person responsible for the protection of personal information above. We will respond within 30 days.

10. Security

We protect personal information with reasonable physical, organizational and technological measures: encrypted transmission (HTTPS), restricted access, password-protected systems and reputable third-party providers. No system is perfectly secure; if a confidentiality incident occurs and presents a risk of serious harm, we will notify the Commission d’accès à l’information and any affected individual as required by Law 25.

11. Children

Our services are intended for adults running businesses. We do not knowingly collect personal information from minors under 14.
